CAM SHOWCASE

The process behind the College of Arts and Media showcase website

The digital design program and the broader College of Arts and Media currently lack a centralized, cohesive digital presence to showcase the exceptional work of their students and alumni. This limits the program's ability to gain public visibility, attract prospective students, and connect students and alumni with industry professionals.

With a collective collaboration with a fellow classmate, Justin Perry, we were assigned to create a showcase website with design solutions that address these issues listed above.

  • To the Prototypes?

Primary User Groups

When considering who the website is going to appeal to, an investigation into who might view this website was a big part of the design process. In order to be successful in the design of the website, Justin and I were able to narrow it down into these user groups.

  • Current art and design students / alumni seeking to showcase their work to peers, instructors, and the broader community.
  • Instructors looking to assess and promote student work, as well as administrators needing to present the program’s achievements.
  • Employers, recruiters, and industry leaders seeking to discover emerging talent for internships, jobs, or collaborations.
  • Individuals considering enrolling in the program who want to explore the caliber and style of student work.
  • Art lovers and community members interested in viewing and engaging with the creative output of students/alumni.

User Goals

One

To help students/alumni gain exposure to industry professionals and recruiters by showcasing their digital portfolios

Two

To learn about the impact of the CAM program, showcasing the curriculum and the effects of the CU Denver digital design program.

Three

To create online engagement and a sense of community

Four

To provide a smooth digital experience through responsive design, user interaction, and clear organization of the navigation bar.

Design Research Conclusions

  • Justin’s Design Research:

    My competitive analysis indicates that a simple but immersive website will create uniqueness in the interface. Elements such as imagery, video, and animations can create a positive user experience. My desk research has indicated a lack of uniqueness can create disassociation within a website. Additionally, a popular reason why users visit other online showcases and collections is for both intellectual and inspiration purposes, either for a visitor’s own research or inspiration.

  • David’s Design Research:

    User interface should be good enough for the user to be able to explore and get to where they want. Creating a space that is simple to navigate is necessary, not only because it keeps the user engaged with the content that they are seeing, but to also make sure that the work being displayed is being showcased appropriately and respectfully.

    Credibility is also a huge factor, as we are showcasing other peoples work and contact information. The goal of this interface is to make sure that it has a purpose and that it accomplishes its goal of user interaction.

    Being able to avoid frustrations, whether it comes from the user or the people being displayed, a set of guidelines and ideas should be laid out. Our main goal is to showcase student work, then our main question is, how can we do that effectively, etc.?
